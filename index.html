Urban Soundscapes

The world is rapidly developing due to technological advancements, leading to easier construction of mega structures and faster modes of commute. However, these advancements also bring new sources of pollution, like sound pollution, which harms the planet and biodiversity. Sound pollution affects birds and is a major reason for declining bird populations in urban areas. Preserving biodiversity is crucial for a sustainable future. An automated system called ChiBa has been proposed to monitor animal sounds and detect changes in their calls, but it requires initial detection of animal calls in the diverse urban environment. Experiments with bird and dog calls in urban areas achieved a high accuracy of 99.91% using a deep learning-based approach with over 7K audio clips.
